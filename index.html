About Me

I’m Ujjwal Singh, a Computer Science and Engineering graduate from Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT). I’m currently building a strong foundation in Machine Learning from first principles, with a deep interest in the mathematics behind intelligent systems. I enjoy understanding how algorithms work rather than simply using them, and I document my learning through technical blogs and projects as I work toward becoming a Machine Learning Engineer.

Why Machine Learning?

Machine Learning fascinated me during my B.Tech, but it always felt like a black box. I knew it was powerful, but I wanted to understand what was happening behind the algorithms instead of simply using them. During my GATE preparation, I studied Linear Algebra and came across Professor Gilbert Strang's MIT OpenCourseWare lectures. His ability to build mathematical intuition from the ground up completely changed the way I approached the subject. For the first time, the theory behind Machine Learning started to make sense, and I realized how closely mathematics and intelligent systems are connected.

That experience inspired me to explore Machine Learning more seriously. I wanted to know whether my interest was genuine curiosity or just temporary excitement, so I kept learning. The deeper I went, the more I enjoyed understanding why algorithms work rather than just how to implement them. Today, I'm focused on building a strong foundation in Machine Learning from first principles while documenting everything I learn through technical blogs and projects. My long term goal is to become a Machine Learning Engineer who combines mathematical understanding with practical problem solving.
